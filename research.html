Research Overview

My research primarily explores the intersection of organizational resilience, systems engineering, and higher education. I am interested in how institutions adapt to complex challenges and how to evaluate and enhance their capabilities using engineering methods.

Current Focus

I am currently working on my doctoral dissertation at Texas Tech University, titled:

“Leveraging Resilience Analysis Grid (RAG) and Functional Resonance Analysis Method (FRAM) for Assessing Organizational Resilience Potential in Higher Education Institutions.”

This project is supported by the National Science Foundation and aims to develop frameworks for understanding and improving how academic institutions respond to disruption and change.

Research Interests

  • Organizational Resilience and Systems Thinking
  • Engineering Education and Identity Formation
  • Human Factors Engineering
  • Performance Measurement and Organizational Assessment
  • Process Improvement in Complex Systems

Methodologies

I use a blend of qualitative and quantitative methods, including:

  • User Experience (UX) methods (e.g., journey mapping, focus groups)
  • Systematic Literature Review (SLR)
  • Survey and interview analysis
  • RAG and FRAM modeling
